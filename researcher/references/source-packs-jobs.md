# Source Pack: Jobs And Career Markets

Load this file for job-market, salary, hiring, skill-gap, or career-path
research.

## 1. Best source classes

Prioritize:
- job descriptions and career pages
- LinkedIn profiles and role trajectories
- salary ranges, compensation reports, and location data
- recruiter or hiring-manager commentary
- practitioner posts about day-to-day work
- GitHub or portfolio artifacts when the role is technical

## 2. What to extract

Look for:
- repeated skill requirements
- seniority mismatches hidden under titles
- regional differences
- adjacent entry paths
- signals that the role is real, inflated, or evolving

For title-sensitive claims, separate:
- `exact-title evidence`
  Roles explicitly using the title in question
- `adjacent-title evidence`
  Roles with different titles but the same capability bundle

Titles often lag the work itself. Do not conclude that a role is unreal merely
because the exact title is rare.

## 3. Challenge paths

Actively test:
- title inflation
- "entry level" roles that still require prior experience
- mismatch between public hype and actual hiring volume
- mismatch between claimed role and daily responsibilities
- mismatch between exact-title scarcity and adjacent-title demand

# Frontier Management

Load this file when you need to score leads, prune branches, or decide what to
deepen next.

## 1. Score branches by evidence value

Score each candidate lead qualitatively against:

1. `Source proximity`
   How close is this branch to a primary, artifact, or otherwise high-value
   source?
2. `Decision relevance`
   If this branch resolves well, does it materially change the answer?
3. `Information gain`
   Will it add new understanding rather than more of the same?
4. `Coverage gap`
   Is this a blind spot in the current map?
5. `Contradiction value`
   Could it falsify or seriously weaken the current thesis?
6. `Incentive insight`
   Will it reveal who is promoting, hiding, or attacking the claim?
7. `Recency value`
   Does the problem depend on current market, product, or hiring conditions?

## 2. Branch types

Use branch types that reflect how evidence moves:

- `Claim branch`
  A specific statement that needs support or falsification.
- `Stakeholder branch`
  A company, lab, recruiter, operator, critic, or customer who matters.
- `Source-gap branch`
  An unresolved need for a missing source class or primary document.
- `Artifact branch`
  Code, docs, dataset, filing, benchmark, repo, product, or legal document.
- `Contradiction branch`
  A rebuttal, short report, complaint, failed case, or competing narrative.
- `Trajectory branch`
  A trend line in hiring, pricing, product motion, regulation, or adoption.

## 3. Selection rules

- Early stage: keep 2-4 active branches to preserve coverage.
- Middle stage: prune branches that no longer change the answer.
- Late stage: converge only after important support and challenge branches were
  both checked.

Do not always follow the highest-scoring supportive branch. Keep at least one
branch alive that could weaken the working thesis.

## 4. Breadth ceiling

The map phase fails when it becomes result hoarding.

Stop widening and build a frontier when:
- the major actors or schools are visible
- the relevant source classes are known
- the main debates are named
- at least one contradiction path exists

Do not confuse:
- many URLs with coverage
- many posts with confirmation
- repeated narratives with independent evidence

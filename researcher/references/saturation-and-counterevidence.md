# Saturation And Counter-Evidence

Load this file when you need stopping rules or when the current thesis feels
too smooth.

## 1. Challenge-path rules

For each important claim, explicitly search for:
- criticism or rebuttal
- failure case
- contradictory user experience
- short report or adversarial analysis
- lawsuit, complaint, or legal dispute
- competing explanation
- evidence that the signal is marketing rather than reality

Treat adversarial sources as lead generators and attack paths. Do not accept
them blindly, but do not ignore them either.

## 2. Minimum challenge coverage

Before final synthesis, ask:
- What source class most disagrees with my current view?
- Which stakeholder has incentives to attack this thesis?
- Which stakeholder has incentives to overstate this thesis?
- Did I check both operator reality and official narrative?
- Did I look for behavioral evidence that either supports or contradicts the
  story?

## 3. Saturation rules

Research is close to done when:
- the main claims are attached to high-value sources
- support and challenge paths were both explored
- the relevant source classes were sampled, not just one ecosystem
- new searches mostly repeat known nodes or low-value variants
- remaining frontier items are peripheral or low-yield

Research is not done when:
- only the supporting story was explored
- community chatter and official narrative sharply disagree without resolution
- the synthesis still depends on snippets or summaries
- new results are only new pages, not new evidence
- no one with opposing incentives was considered
